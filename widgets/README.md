\# AQUAL Widget System — Integral Pragmatism Writing Layer



\## What This Is



The AQUAL widget system is a two-stage writing aid developed for

DoughForge and the reproducible-self-pub-kit. It combines:



1\. \*\*AQAL Screener\*\* — Ken Wilber's All Quadrants All Levels framework

&nbsp;  used as a bias-detection instrument before drafting any passage.

2\. \*\*Voice Widgets\*\* — rhetorical style prompts modelled on specific

&nbsp;  writers, selectable based on the register the passage requires.



Together they form an \*\*Integral Pragmatism\*\* layer: you check what

you are seeing (AQAL), then choose how to say it (voice widget).



\## Theoretical Basis



The system draws on two intellectual traditions that rarely meet:



\*\*Ken Wilber's AQAL\*\* maps every phenomenon across four quadrants:

\- \*\*I\*\* — interior-individual (intention, experience, phenomenology)

\- \*\*WE\*\* — interior-collective (culture, shared meaning, worldview)

\- \*\*IT\*\* — exterior-individual (behaviour, observable fact, evidence)

\- \*\*ITS\*\* — exterior-collective (systems, institutions, ecology)



Every argument has a quadrant bias. Most arguments have two or three

quadrants missing entirely. The screener makes that bias visible

before it becomes invisible prose.



\*\*C.S. Peirce's Pragmatic Maxim\*\* asks of every claim:

\*"What practical difference would it make if this were true rather

than false?"\* If the answer is none, the sentence is decorative.

If the answer is significant, identify which quadrant carries the

consequence — and make sure that quadrant is present in the prose.



\## The Unacknowledged Legislators Connection



Shelley's claim — "poets are the unacknowledged legislators of the

world" — is an AQAL WE-quadrant assertion: that the interior-

collective (culture, meaning, shared vision) is the actual driver

of exterior-collective change (systems, institutions, laws).



The integral pragmatism layer operationalises this. Blake's prophetic

register inhabits the WE quadrant — he writes as though the cultural

vision \*is\* the political act. Peirce's semiotic register inhabits

the ITS quadrant — he writes as though the sign system \*is\* the

social infrastructure. Neither is wrong. Both are partial.



The AQAL screener is what stops you from writing an entire book from

one quadrant while believing you are covering the whole.



As the \*Unacknowledged Legislators\* puts it:



> "The unacknowledged legislators do not pass laws. They alter the

> interpretants — the meanings that make certain laws thinkable and

> others unimaginable. This is Peirce's semiotic read of Shelley's

> claim, and it is more radical than either of them intended."



\## How to Use



1\. Run `aqual\_screener/worldview\_audit.md` on your draft passage.

2\. Identify which quadrant is underrepresented.

3\. Select a voice widget that naturally inhabits that quadrant.

4\. Redraft the passage through that voice.

5\. Composite if needed — see `compositor.md`.



\## Fork Invitation



This widget system is designed to be extended. If you fork

`DoughForge` or `reproducible-self-pub-kit`, add your own voice

widgets under `widgets/voice\_widgets/`. The only requirement: each

widget file should specify which AQAL quadrant(s) the voice

naturally inhabits, and include a prompt template.



Current voice widgets:

\- `will\_cuppy.md` — deadpan footnote satirist (IT + I)

\- `cs\_peirce.md` — semiotic pragmatist (ITS + IT)

\- `john\_ruskin.md` — moral aesthetic critic (WE + ITS)

\- `william\_blake.md` — prophetic visionary (WE + I)

\- `percy\_shelley.md` — radical lyric idealist (WE + I)

\- `\[add\_your\_own].md` — fork invitation
