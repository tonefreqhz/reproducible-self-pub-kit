### WHO IS JOHN WARD?

John Anthony Ward — known online as **The Slog** — is a retired British advertising and PR professional
who spent his career at the top of the communications industry, including as CEO and Deputy Chairman
of publicly quoted agencies. Educated at grammar school and Liverpool University (History & Politics),
he developed a parallel expertise in social anthropology through decades of communications research —
an expertise he found conspicuously absent in every politician he ever worked with.

He began blogging in 2007. He has lived in southwest France and, latterly, The Gambia.
His writing is archived at **therealslog.ghost.io** (current) and **hat4uk.wordpress.com** (archive).

His tagline: **#IABATO — It's All Bollocks And That's Official.**
His method: deconstruct the bollocks. Every time. Without exception.

### THE VOICE — WHAT MAKES WARD DISTINCTIVE

Ward writes and speaks from a position that is:

- **Post-ideological** — neither left nor right, but empirical. He will call out Tories, Labour,
  Socialists, Islamists, and Globalists with equal vigour if the evidence demands it.
- **Anthropologically grounded** — his core thesis is that human beings are a hierarchical,
  tribal species, and that any political system which ignores this will breed corruption.
- **Commercially literate** — he spent 40 years watching markets, clients, and governments
  make the same category error: believing you can perfect human beings through systems.
- **Mordantly funny** — the humour is dry, self-aware, and never punches down.

His recurring targets:
- The unelected state ("the deep state" — though he prefers "the unaccountable")
- The financialisation of capitalism
- Process politics over policy substance
- Digital surveillance and the coercive internet
- The DWP and pension theft from 1950s-born women (WASPI)
- The 4% sociopathic elite vs the 96%

### THE 2019 ELECTION NIGHT INTERVIEW — ROGER LEWIS & JOHN WARD

**Broadcast live:** 12 December 2019, 7pm GMT
**Platform:** YouTube / Grub Street Journal / Four Pamphleteers
**Interviewer:** Roger Lewis (Porthos)

This interview is the canonical record of Ward's political analysis in real time,
on the night of the 2019 UK General Election.

#### Key Exchange — Question by Question

**Q1 — Biography**
Ward's career arc: grammar school → Liverpool University → advertising PLC CEO →
Deputy Chairman → communications research → blogging. The pivot point: realising
that politicians had *zero* understanding of anthropology, and that focus groups
in the hands of politicians were "like giving an unexploded bomb to a child."

**Q2 — Is Boris / Get Brexit Done credible?**
Short answer: No. Reason: Mark Sedwill — the Permanent Secretary and Cabinet Office
eminence grise — was still in post. "As long as he stays in place, nothing Boris says
is credible to me." The unelected state had not been disturbed.

**Q3 — Real issues vs narrative talking points**
The election was *built* as a Brexit election but *discussed* almost nothing of substance.
Real issues ignored: WASPI pensions, accountability, equality before the law, the coming
financial crash. "Everybody in the markets knows it — they're just milking the cow until
the poor devil falls over."

**Q4 — Process politics**
Ward's definition: a conformist, ideological way of doing things that closes minds rather
than opening them. The antithesis of creative thinking. Applies equally to the hard right,
international socialism, and political Islam. "The defence of small-scale entrepreneurial
philosophy against big business process — to use the technical term — shit."
The Slog's name origin: a former Telegraph editor told him he was "a bollocks deconstructor."
He called it the Bollocks Log. Then shortened it.

**Q5 — Turnout and majority prediction**
Queues at every polling station across Britain. Ward revised upward from his earlier
59% estimate. On majority: "There will be a Tory majority of at least thirty seats.
If the undecideds held their nose... it could actually be ninety-six."
Gut feeling: 25–45 seat working majority. *Actual result: 80 seat majority.*
Ward also called it: "The gloves will be off in the Labour Party the day after tomorrow."

**Q6 — Slogan for the election**
*"Empty."*
Empty of ideas. Empty of morals. Empty of any solution for the greatest fulfilment
of the greatest number. "It has been an empty election."

**Q7 — Farage and tactical voting**
Farage stood down candidates in Tory-held seats. Ward's view: the Brexit Party's
role was structurally significant but Farage's execution was characteristically
self-defeating. The real tactical story was the 27% undecided — a record —
which the MSM largely ignored.

**Q8 — Looking back: 2010, 2015, Brexit, 2017**
- **2010:** Brown's Brownites upbeat, refusing to see the writing on the wall.
  Ward's read: the deficit was accelerating, the credit agencies would not be fooled.
- **2015:** The shock Tory majority. Ward's autopsy: the oldest adage of market research —
  people lie to pollsters about how they'll vote when they're embarrassed to admit it.
- **2016 Brexit:** "Victory — but we won the first battle, not the war."
- **2017:** "The fakelection nobody won — but Brexit lost bigtime."
