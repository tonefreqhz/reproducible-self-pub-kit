\# AQAL Worldview Audit — Pre-Draft Screener



Before writing, answer these four questions about your passage.

Each maps to one quadrant of Wilber's AQAL framework.



---



\## The Four Questions



\### I — Interior Individual (Intentional)

\*What does the individual feel, intend, or experience here?\*

\- Am I representing the inner life of the person or position?

\- Is there a phenomenological dimension I am ignoring?

\- Voice widgets that inhabit this quadrant:

&nbsp; \*\*Blake, Shelley, Cuppy (self-deprecating register)\*\*



\### WE — Interior Collective (Cultural)

\*What shared meanings, values, or worldviews are at play?\*

\- Am I accounting for the cultural context that makes this

&nbsp; argument legible?

\- Whose "we" am I assuming?

\- Voice widgets: \*\*Ruskin, Blake (prophetic register)\*\*



\### IT — Exterior Individual (Behavioural)

\*What observable behaviour or measurable fact is relevant?\*

\- Am I grounding the argument in something that can be pointed to?

\- Is the claim falsifiable or at least demonstrable?

\- Voice widgets: \*\*Peirce, Cuppy (footnote-evidence register)\*\*



\### ITS — Exterior Collective (Systems)

\*What institutional, economic, or ecological system is operating?\*

\- Am I seeing the structural forces, not just the individuals?

\- Is the "circle of blame" actually a systems problem?

\- Voice widgets: \*\*Peirce (semiotic systems), Ruskin (political economy)\*\*



---



\## Audit Checklist



\- \[ ] I quadrant represented?

\- \[ ] WE quadrant represented?

\- \[ ] IT quadrant represented?

\- \[ ] ITS quadrant represented?

\- \[ ] Dominant quadrant identified and intentional?

\- \[ ] Suppressed quadrant addressed or consciously set aside?



---



\## Peirce Pragmatic Check



After the quadrant audit, apply the Pragmatic Maxim:



> \*"Consider what effects, that might conceivably have practical

> bearings, we conceive the object of our conception to have.

> Then, our conception of these effects is the whole of our

> conception of the object."\*

> — C.S. Peirce, \*How to Make Our Ideas Clear\*, 1878



If the practical consequence of your claim is "none" — the passage

is decorative. If it is "significant" — identify which quadrant

carries the consequence and make sure that quadrant is present

in the prose.
